Alpha Mining for Predictive Stock Returns

- Fundamental Analysis
  - Price Action Indicators
    - Simple Moving Averages (SMA): Calculate SMA over different time windows to identify trend changes.
      - Difference between short and long term SMAs: Identify crossover points as buy/sell signals.
  - Volume-Based Signals
    - On-Balance Volume (OBV): Measure buying and selling pressure through volume.
      - OBV Direction Change: Signal when OBV starts moving in the opposite direction of the price, suggesting a potential reversal.
- Technical Indicators 
  - Momentum Oscillators
    - Relative Strength Index (RSI): Evaluate overbought/oversold conditions using RSI without smoothing or normalization.
      - Divergence with Price: Look for RSI not confirming new highs/lows in price, indicating weakening momentum.
  - Volatility Measures
    - Average True Range (ATR) over various periods: Assess volatility.
      - ATR Changes: Increase in ATR may signal upcoming significant moves.
- Pattern Recognition
  - Price Patterns
    - Inside and Outside Bars: Recognize patterns that indicate consolidation or breakout phases.
- Statistical Models
  - Linear Regression Model
    - Slope: Compute the slope of a regression line fitted on close prices over a specific period.
      - Change in Slope: Monitor for inflection points where the trend potentially reverses.
- Custom Alphas
  - Combined Signals
    - Conjunction of Momentum and Volume: Develop a score by combining signals from RSI divergence and OBV direction changes.
  - Proprietary Indicators
    - Construct a unique combination of price action, volume, and volatility indicators.
      - Test across multiple securities to ensure robustness and broad applicability.
