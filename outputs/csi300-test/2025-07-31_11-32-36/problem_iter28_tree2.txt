```txt
- Root: Explore price and volume dynamics to generate novel alpha factors
  - Price Trend Analysis
    - Calculate the difference between today's close and yesterday's close to identify daily price movement
    - Use the difference of high and low over a period to measure volatility
      - High minus Low for each day
      - Average True Range (ATR) without normalization
  - Volume Impact on Prices
    - Analyze the correlation between volume change and price change
      - Volume change from yesterday to today
      - Corresponding price change
    - Calculate the ratio of price change to volume change
  - Momentum Indicators
    - Sum of closing prices over a short term (e.g., 5 days)
    - Sum of closing prices over a long term (e.g., 20 days)
    - Difference between the short-term and long-term sums to gauge momentum
  - Support and Resistance Levels
    - Identify recent high and low prices
    - Count the number of times the price has tested these levels
  - Breakout Potential
    - Measure the distance between the current price and the highest high in the past N days
    - Measure the distance between the current price and the lowest low in the past N days
  - Accumulation and Distribution
    - Calculate the money flow multiplier using (close - low) / (high - low)
    - Multiply the money flow multiplier by the volume to get money flow volume
    - Sum the money flow volumes over a specified period
  - Volatility and Risk
    - Calculate the standard deviation of the closing prices over a period
    - Calculate the range of the closing prices over a period
  - Seasonal and Cyclical Patterns
    - Analyze the historical data for patterns that repeat at specific intervals
    - Identify the average return for each interval
  - Relative Strength
    - Compare the stock's performance with a benchmark index
    - Calculate the ratio of the stock's return to the benchmark's return
  - Price and Volume Ratios
    - Calculate the ratio of the sum of closing prices to the sum of volumes
    - Use this ratio to identify potential undervalued or overvalued conditions
```
