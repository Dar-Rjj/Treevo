High-Low Spread with Volume Trend and Relative Strength
├── Calculate High-Low Spread
│   ├── Use High price
│   └── Use Low price
│       └── Subtract Low from High
├── Calculate Daily Volume Trend
│   ├── Use Volume
│   │   └── Calculate 10-day weighted moving average of volume
│   └── Subtract today's volume from 10-day weighted moving average
│       └── Determine if positive or negative trend
│           ├── If (today's volume - 10-day weighted moving average) > 0, positive trend
│           └── If (today's volume - 10-day weighted moving average) < 0, negative trend
├── Calculate Relative Strength
│   ├── Calculate 14-day relative strength
│   │   ├── Sum of gains over 14 days
│   │   └── Sum of losses over 14 days
│   └── Calculate relative strength index (RSI)
│       └── RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + (average gain / average loss)))
└── Combine Spread, Volume Trend, and Relative Strength
    ├── Multiply High-Low Spread by Volume Trend
    │   ├── If Volume Trend is positive, multiply by 1.5
    │   └── If Volume Trend is negative, multiply by 0.5
    ├── Adjust for Relative Strength
    │   ├── If RSI > 70, decrease the factor by 20%
    │   └── If RSI < 30, increase the factor by 20%
    └── Final Alpha Factor
        ├── Use the adjusted spread as the alpha factor
        └── Interpretation: 
            ├── Higher value indicates potential for higher returns
            └── Lower value indicates potential for lower returns
