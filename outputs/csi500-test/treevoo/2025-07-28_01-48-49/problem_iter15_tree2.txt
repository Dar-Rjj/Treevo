Alpha Mining for Predicting Future Stock Returns
- Momentum-based Alpha Factors
  - Price Momentum
    - Calculate the return from t-n to t (e.g., n=20, 50, 100)
  - Volume Adjusted Momentum
    - Combine price momentum with a volume change factor from t-n to t
- Trend Following Alpha Factors
  - Moving Average Crossover
    - Short-term moving average (e.g., 5-day) over long-term moving average (e.g., 20-day)
  - High-Low Breakout
    - Identify if today's high exceeds the highest high of the past n days or if today's low is below the lowest low of the past n days
- Market Sentiment Indicators
  - Cumulative Return in the Last n Days
    - Aggregate return calculated as the sum of daily returns over the last n days
  - Close-to-Open Gap
    - Measure the gap between the current open and the previous close
- Volume Analysis
  - Volume Surge
    - Compare the current day's volume against the average volume over the previous n days to identify significant volume changes
  - Volume Oscillator
    - Use the difference between short-term and long-term moving averages of volume
- Relative Strength Comparisons
  - Sector/Industry Relative Performance
    - Compare the performance of the stock to its sector/industry over the past n days
  - Benchmark Relative Performance
    - Compare the stock's performance to a benchmark index over the past n days
- Technical Pattern Recognition
  - Bullish/Bearish Engulfing
    - Identify if today's candlestick fully engulfs the previous day's, indicating a potential trend reversal
  - Doji Star
    - Detect the presence of a doji star pattern, which can signal indecision in the market
- Order Flow Imbalance
  - Bid-Ask Spread
    - Measure the difference between the bid and ask prices to gauge liquidity and market sentiment
  - Trade Imbalance
    - Evaluate the imbalance between the number of buy and sell orders at key price levels
- Fundamental Data Integration
  - Earnings Surprise
    - Factor in the impact of earnings reports exceeding or falling short of expectations
  - Dividend Yield
    - Consider the dividend yield as an indicator of the stockâ€™s attractiveness to income-focused investors
- Seasonality and Calendar Effects
  - Month-of-Year Effect
    - Analyze the historical performance of the stock in different months to predict future trends
  - Day-of-Week Effect
    - Examine the typical performance of the stock on specific days of the week
