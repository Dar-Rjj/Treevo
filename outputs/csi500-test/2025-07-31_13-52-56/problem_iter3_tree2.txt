Price Momentum, Volume Shock, and Price Volatility Factor
├── Calculate Price Momentum
│   ├── Use Close prices
│   │   ├── Get the close price of day t
│   │   └── Get the close price of day t-30
│   └── Compute the 30-day Momentum
│       ├── Subtract close price of day t-30 from close price of day t
│       └── Divide the result by the close price of day t-30
├── Identify Volume Shock
│   ├── Use Volume data
│   │   ├── Get the volume of day t
│   │   └── Get the average volume of the last 30 days (excluding day t)
│   └── Determine if a shock occurred
│       ├── Compare day t volume with 30-day average volume
│       └── Assign value: 1 if day t volume > 2 * 30-day average, else 0
├── Calculate Price Volatility
│   ├── Use Close prices
│   │   ├── Get the close prices of the last 30 days (including day t)
│   └── Compute the 30-day Volatility
│       ├── Calculate the standard deviation of the 30-day close prices
└── Combine Momentum, Volume Shock, and Price Volatility
    ├── Multiply the 30-day Momentum by the Volume Shock indicator
    ├── Adjust the combined factor by the 30-day Volatility
    │   └── Divide the product of Momentum and Volume Shock by the 30-day Volatility
    └── Interpretation: A high positive or negative value indicates a strong price momentum accompanied by a significant volume surge and adjusted for volatility, potentially signaling a continuation trend with risk consideration.
