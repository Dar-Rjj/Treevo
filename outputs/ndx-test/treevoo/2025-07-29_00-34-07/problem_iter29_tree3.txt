```txt
[Root]
- [Momentum]
  - [Short-term Momentum]
    - Calculate the average of closing prices over the past 5 days.
    - Subtract the 20-day moving average of closing prices from the 5-day average.
  - [Long-term Momentum]
    - Calculate the 100-day moving average of closing prices.
    - Subtract the 200-day moving average of closing prices from the 100-day moving average.
  - [Relative Momentum]
    - Compare the current close price to the 5-day, 20-day, and 100-day moving averages.
- [Volatility]
  - [Price Volatility]
    - Calculate the standard deviation of high-minus-low prices over the past 10 days.
  - [Volume Volatility]
    - Calculate the standard deviation of volume over the past 10 days.
  - [Combined Volatility]
    - Combine the high-minus-low volatility and volume volatility by taking their geometric mean.
- [Trend]
  - [Short-term Trend]
    - Calculate the slope of a linear regression line for the past 30 days' closing prices.
  - [Long-term Trend]
    - Calculate the slope of a linear regression line for the past 120 days' closing prices.
- [Strength]
  - [Relative Strength]
    - Compute the ratio of the current close price to the 52-week high price.
  - [Earnings Strength]
    - Use the earnings announcement date to mark periods. Calculate the difference between the average closing price before and after the earnings announcement.
- [Crossover]
  - [Moving Average Crossover]
    - Check if the 50-day moving average crosses above the 200-day moving average.
  - [MACD Crossover]
    - Compute the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) using the 12-day and 26-day EMAs.
    - Check if the MACD line crosses above the 9-day signal line.
- [Reversal]
  - [Overbought/Oversold]
    - Use the RSI (Relative Strength Index) to determine if the stock is overbought or oversold.
  - [Bollinger Bands]
    - Calculate Bollinger Bands with a 20-day moving average and 2 standard deviations.
    - Check if the close price is outside the upper or lower Bollinger Band.
- [Sentiment]
  - [News Sentiment]
    - Summarize the sentiment score of news articles related to the stock over the past 7 days.
  - [Option Implied Volatility]
    - Use the implied volatility from options data as a proxy for market sentiment.
- [Liquidity]
  - [Turnover Rate]
    - Calculate the ratio of trading volume to the total number of outstanding shares over the past 10 days.
  - [Volume Price Correlation]
    - Calculate the correlation between the daily volume and the daily close price over the past 30 days.
- [Seasonality]
  - [Monthly Seasonality]
    - Calculate the average monthly return for each month of the year over the past 5 years.
  - [Quarterly Earnings Season]
    - Identify the months when the company typically reports earnings and calculate the average return during those months.
- [Event Driven]
  - [Dividend Announcement]
    - Mark the dates of dividend announcements and calculate the average return in the 10 days following the announcement.
  - [Stock Splits]
    - Mark the dates of stock splits and calculate the average return in the 10 days following the split.
- [Technical Patterns]
  - [Head and Shoulders]
    - Identify the formation of a head and shoulders pattern and its implications.
  - [Double Top/Bottom]
    - Identify the formation of a double top or bottom pattern and its implications.
- [Market Conditions]
  - [Market Sentiment]
    - Use the VIX (CBOE Volatility Index) as a proxy for market sentiment.
  - [Sector Performance]
    - Calculate the relative performance of the stock's sector compared to the overall market.
- [Economic Indicators]
  - [Interest Rates]
    - Use changes in the Federal Funds Rate as an indicator of economic conditions.
  - [Inflation]
    - Use the Consumer Price Index (CPI) as an indicator of inflation and its impact on the stock.
- [Corporate Actions]
  - [Share Repurchases]
    - Identify the dates of share repurchase announcements and calculate the average return in the 10 days following the announcement.
  - [Merger and Acquisition]
    - Identify the dates of M&A announcements and calculate the average return in the 10 days following the announcement.
```
